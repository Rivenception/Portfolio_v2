Education is the cornerstone of success.


Throughout my time at university, I’ve had the opportunity to dive into a variety of subjects that have shaped my knowledge and skills. Here’s a big list of all of the relevant classes at university that I've taken.
Software Development
CSC 545: Advanced Database Systems
In this class, I really got into the complexities of large-scale database management. I learned how to optimize data retrieval and design efficient, high-performance databases. It was a deep dive into advanced querying and performance tuning techniques.

CSC 543: Web Programming
I picked up the core skills of web development, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and backend technologies. Building interactive and dynamic websites became second nature. This class really helped me understand both the front-end and back-end sides of web programming.

CSC 540: Database Systems
This course taught me the essentials of relational database design and management. I got a solid understanding of SQL, data modeling, and how to structure data for efficient retrieval. It gave me the foundation I needed to work with complex database systems.

CSC 535: Software Engineering
Here, I learned about the software development lifecycle, focusing on making the process more efficient and ensuring quality at every stage. I worked on everything from requirements gathering to testing, learning how to manage projects and create maintainable software.

CSC 521: Algorithms
In this class, I mastered various problem-solving techniques and algorithms. I learned how to analyze and optimize algorithms to ensure they perform well, especially with large datasets. It really sharpened my ability to approach problems with the most efficient solution.

CSC 503: Computer Systems Networking
I gained a solid understanding of how computer networks operate, from data transmission to troubleshooting. We covered essential networking protocols and security, and I learned how to manage and protect networks effectively.

CSC 200: Information Management/Productivity Software
This course helped me become more efficient with productivity software like spreadsheets and databases. I learned how to manage and organize information, which greatly improved my ability to work with data in a business or academic setting.

Economics
ECO 398: Law and Economics
This class gave me insight into how legal systems impact economic behavior. I explored topics like property rights, contracts, and regulations, learning how law and economics intertwine to shape decision-making and policy.

ECO 340: Money and Banking
I learned how financial institutions and central banks drive the economy. This class focused on monetary policy and the role of money in economic stability and growth, helping me understand how banking systems influence the broader economy.

ECO 311: Public Finance
In this course, I explored how governments handle their budgets and taxation. I learned about the economic implications of public spending and how fiscal policy affects national and global economies.

ECO 304: Labor Economics
This class helped me understand labor markets and the factors that influence wages and employment. I explored how supply and demand shape the labor force and the broader economic effects of unemployment and income distribution.

ECO 221: Statistics for Economics and Business
I learned how to apply statistical methods to real-world economic and business data. The course helped me analyze trends and make informed decisions based on quantitative evidence, which is crucial in both economics and business.

ECO 201: Micro Economic Analysis
Through this class, I gained a deeper understanding of individual economic agents like consumers and firms. I learned about market structures, pricing strategies, and the factors that influence supply and demand on a smaller scale.

ECO 200: Macro Economic Analysis
This class introduced me to the broader economic forces at play on a national and global scale. I studied concepts like inflation, unemployment, and fiscal policy, and learned how these variables interact to affect overall economic health.

Philosophy
PHI 324: Ethics and AI
This class really made me think about the ethical challenges of artificial intelligence. I explored topics like AI decision-making, bias, and the responsibility of developers in creating fair, transparent systems. It was a fascinating look at the moral implications of technology.

PHI 215: Modal Logic
In this course, I learned how to reason about possibilities and necessities using formal logic. I gained an understanding of how to structure arguments about what could or must be true, which sharpened my critical thinking skills.

PHI 200: Problems in Philosophy
This class was a great introduction to some of philosophy’s biggest questions. I explored everything from the nature of reality to ethical dilemmas, learning how to think critically about fundamental issues and challenge assumptions.

PHI 120: Ethics
I really enjoyed this course because it allowed me to dive deep into moral philosophy. I explored different ethical theories and applied them to real-world situations, helping me develop a more nuanced view of right and wrong in everyday life.
